Vehicle Fire

Emergency Preparedness Tips:

  • Regularly maintain your vehicle and address any mechanical issues promptly.
  • Keep a fire extinguisher rated for vehicle fires in your car and know how to use it.
  • Avoid smoking inside the vehicle, especially while driving.
  • Keep flammable materials and substances away from heat sources in the vehicle.
  • Park your vehicle in safe and designated areas, away from flammable materials.

What to Do During a Vehicle Fire:

  • Safely pull over and park the vehicle away from traffic, if possible.
  • Turn off the engine and remove the key.
  • Evacuate all occupants of the vehicle and move to a safe distance.
  • Do not attempt to open the hood or put out the fire yourself.
  • Call emergency services and report the vehicle fire.

After a Vehicle Fire:

  • Do not re-enter or attempt to move the vehicle until it has been declared safe by the authorities.
  • Seek necessary medical attention for any injuries sustained during the incident.
  • Contact your insurance provider to report the vehicle fire.
  • Cooperate with fire investigators if necessary to determine the cause of the fire.
  • Take steps to prevent future vehicle fires, such as regular vehicle maintenance and inspections.
