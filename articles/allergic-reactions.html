Allergic Reactions

Symptoms of Allergic Reactions:

  • Hives or rash
  • Swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Chest tightness
  • Dizziness or fainting
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Abdominal pain or cramps
  • Itchy or watery eyes
  • Runny or stuffy nose

What to Do for Allergic Reactions:

  • If someone is experiencing a severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis), call emergency services immediately.
  • Help the person use their prescribed epinephrine auto-injector (e.g., EpiPen) if available.
  • Assist the person in lying down and elevating their legs, if possible.
  • Loosen any tight clothing and cover them with a blanket to keep them warm.
  • Reassure the person and try to keep them calm.
  • Monitor their vital signs, such as pulse and breathing rate.

When to Seek Medical Help:

  • If the person is having difficulty breathing or their symptoms are worsening.
  • If the person has a known severe allergy and has used their epinephrine auto-injector.
  • If the person has lost consciousness or shows signs of severe distress.

After the Allergic Reaction:

  • Stay with the person until medical help arrives.
  • Provide any relevant medical history or information to the healthcare professionals.
  • Follow any further instructions or recommendations given by medical professionals.
  • Encourage the person to see an allergist for further evaluation and management of their allergies.
