Seizures

Understanding Seizures:

A seizure is a sudden, uncontrolled electrical disturbance in the brain that can cause changes in behavior, movements, sensations, or awareness. It is often associated with convulsions or shaking movements.

What to Do During a Seizure:

  • Stay calm and try to keep the person safe.
  • Clear the area of any objects that could cause harm.
  • Do not restrain the person or put anything in their mouth.
  • Time the seizure and note any unusual behaviors or movements.
  • After the seizure, help the person into a safe position and provide comfort and reassurance.

When to Seek Medical Help:

  • If it is the person's first seizure or if the seizure lasts longer than 5 minutes.
  • If the person is injured during the seizure.
  • If the person has difficulty breathing or remains unconscious after the seizure.
  • If the person has multiple seizures without regaining consciousness in between.

After a Seizure:

  • Stay with the person until they are fully alert and oriented.
  • Offer support and reassurance.
  • Help the person rest and recover.
  • If necessary, assist with medical follow-up or transportation to a healthcare facility.
