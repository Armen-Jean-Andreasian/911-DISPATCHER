Victim of Trafficking

Preparation:

  1. Before every trip, make sure to check three important numbers:
    • The phone number of your embassy, consulate, and the responsible person.
    • The phone number of the police and ambulance.
    • The phone number of international human rights organizations in the country you are visiting.
  2. If someone takes your passport in a foreign country, it's NOT A PROBLEM at all. They want you to think that you can't return without a passport, but that's not true. Contact your embassy, and they will provide you with a temporary passport, which will allow you to return. Even if you don't have money to return, your embassy can contact your government, which can arrange a free ticket for you.
  3. Never go to isolated places with locals if you are a tourist.
  4. Always leave trails behind you. Stay in touch with your friends online, take photos, and share them on social media. Try to download a taxi app, as it can provide important details about your trip and the driver's name.

Actions in Case of Being a Victim:

  1. Check the area, your location, collect information, and analyze it.
  2. Try to escape using the following strategies:
    • Stay Calm and Assess the Situation: Maintain composure and gather information about your surroundings, captors, and potential escape routes.
    • Identify Safe Opportunities: Look for moments when you might be less closely monitored or when captors' attention is diverted.
    • Scenario 1: Normal country with normal police:
      • Establish Allies: If possible, build trust with other victims or sympathetic individuals who can assist you during your escape.
      • Seek Help from Authorities: Contact the local police or trustworthy law enforcement agencies and report your situation. Provide them with evidence or information to support your claims.
      • Contact Your Embassy or Consulate: Reach out to your country's embassy or consulate in the country you're in. They can provide guidance, support, and facilitate your return home.
    • Scenario 2: Corrupt Country with police involved in organized crime:
      • Prioritize Safety and Discretion: Recognize the risks associated with seeking help from corrupt authorities. Proceed with caution and consider alternative options, if possible.
      • If possible:
        • Contact International Human Rights Organizations: Reach out to international human rights organizations operating in the country or those experienced in handling trafficking cases. They may provide assistance or connect you with local resources.
        • Seek Assistance from NGOs and Safe Houses: Look for non-governmental organizations (NGOs) or safe houses specialized in helping trafficking victims. They can navigate the challenges posed by corrupt authorities.
        • Maintain Discreet Communication: Use secure communication methods to contact trusted individuals, organizations, or hotlines that offer guidance and support.
      • Plan a Covert Escape: Avoid sharing escape plans with anyone, especially with those who are suspected of involvement in trafficking or corruption.

Escape Strategies:

  1. Stay Calm and Assess the Situation:
    • Maintain composure and try to gather as much information as possible about your surroundings, captors, and any potential opportunities for escape.
  2. Identify Safe Opportunities:
    • Look for moments when you might be less closely monitored or when your captors' attention is diverted.
    • Assess the environment for potential escape routes or areas where you may find help or resources.
  3. Prioritize Your Safety:
    • Consider your personal safety as the utmost priority throughout the escape process.
    • Assess potential risks and plan accordingly to minimize the chances of detection or harm.
  4. Plan a Covert Escape:
    • Don't believe anyone, don't share your plan with others.
    • Consider factors such as timing, routes, transportation options, and potential risks.
  5. Gather Essential Items:
    • Prepare necessary documents, identification, money, and any other essential items that will aid your escape, including self-defense tools if legally allowed.
    • Keep these items hidden and secure, ensuring easy access when needed.
    • Consider Self-Defense:
      • Think about self-defense techniques and strategies that can help you protect yourself in dangerous situations.
      • Attend self-defense classes or seek guidance from experts who can teach you practical skills.
    • Collecting Weapons:
      • If you feel it is necessary for your safety, consider collecting self-defense tools.
      • Remember, weapons should only be used as a last resort to protect your life when there is an immediate threat. Use them responsibly and with caution.
  6. Use Discreet Communication:
    • Maintain discreet communication with trusted individuals, organizations, or hotlines that can offer guidance and support.
    • Utilize secure communication methods, such as encrypted messaging apps or anonymous phone lines.
  7. Seek Assistance from Authorities:
    • If you are in a country with trustworthy authorities, contact the local police or law enforcement agencies to report your situation.
    • Provide them with any evidence or information that can support your claims.
  8. Reach Out to Support Organizations:
    • Contact international human rights organizations operating in the country or those experienced in handling trafficking cases.
    • They may provide assistance or connect you with local resources.
    • Seek assistance from non-governmental organizations (NGOs) or safe houses specialized in helping trafficking victims.
  9. Contact Your Embassy or Consulate:
    • Reach out to your country's embassy or consulate in the country you're in.
    • They can provide guidance, support, and potentially facilitate your return home.
    • Inform them about your situation and seek their assistance in ensuring your safety.

Remember, every situation is unique, and it's important to assess the risks and resources available to you. Trust your instincts and seek help from reliable sources. Your safety and well-being are paramount.
