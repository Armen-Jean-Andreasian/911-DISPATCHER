Building Fire

Emergency Preparedness Tips:

  • Install smoke detectors on every level of your home and regularly test them.
  • Create and practice a fire escape plan with all household members.
  • Keep flammable materials, such as curtains and furniture, away from heat sources.
  • Have a fire extinguisher readily available and know how to use it.
  • Teach everyone in your household how to stop, drop, and roll in case their clothing catches fire.

What to Do During a Building Fire:

  • Stay low to the ground to avoid smoke inhalation.
  • Find the nearest exit and evacuate the building immediately.
  • If you encounter smoke while escaping, use an alternative route if available.
  • Do not use elevators during a fire; use the stairs instead.
  • Alert others and help those who may require assistance to evacuate.

After a Building Fire:

  • Do not re-enter the building until it has been declared safe by the authorities.
  • Contact your insurance company and report the incident.
  • Assess and document the damages for insurance purposes.
  • Seek necessary medical attention for injuries.
  • Take steps to prevent future fires, such as reviewing fire safety practices and making necessary changes.
