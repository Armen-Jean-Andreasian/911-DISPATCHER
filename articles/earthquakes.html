Earthquakes

Emergency Preparedness Tips:

  • Secure heavy furniture and objects to prevent them from falling and causing injuries.
  • Create an emergency kit with essential supplies such as water, food, flashlight, and first aid supplies.
  • Identify safe spots in your home or workplace where you can take cover during an earthquake.
  • Practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" - Drop to the ground, take cover under a sturdy piece of furniture, and hold on until the shaking stops.
  • Stay away from windows, glass, and other items that may shatter during an earthquake.
  • Have a plan for communication and reunion with family members in case of separation during an earthquake.

What to Do During an Earthquake:

  • Drop to the ground, take cover under a sturdy piece of furniture, and hold on until the shaking stops.
  • If you are unable to take cover, protect your head and neck with your arms and seek shelter against an interior wall away from windows.
  • Avoid using elevators during an earthquake.
  • If you are outdoors, move to an open area away from buildings, trees, streetlights, and utility wires.

After an Earthquake:

  • Check yourself and others for injuries and provide first aid if necessary.
  • Check for hazards such as gas leaks, damaged electrical wires, or structural damage. If you suspect any, evacuate the area and contact emergency services.
  • Listen to the radio or check official sources for updates and instructions from local authorities.
  • Be prepared for aftershocks and take necessary precautions.
