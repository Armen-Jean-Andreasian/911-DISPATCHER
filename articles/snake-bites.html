Snake Bites

Symptoms of Snake Bites:

  • Puncture wounds or bite marks
  • Swelling and redness around the bite area
  • Pain or burning sensation
  • Bleeding or oozing from the wound
  • Blurred vision or drooping eyelids
  • Dizziness or fainting
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Weakness or difficulty breathing

What to Do for Snake Bites:

  • Keep calm and try to move away from the snake.
  • Call emergency services immediately and provide them with details about the snake if possible.
  • Keep the bitten area below the heart level to slow the spread of venom.
  • Remove any constricting items like jewelry or clothing from the affected limb.
  • Do not try to suck out the venom or make any cuts on the wound.
  • Apply a clean, sterile bandage or cloth over the wound to control bleeding.
  • Keep the person still and avoid unnecessary movement to prevent venom from spreading.
  • Monitor the person's vital signs, such as pulse and breathing rate.

When to Seek Medical Help:

  • Always seek medical help for snake bites, regardless of the snake's type or size.
  • If the person is experiencing severe symptoms, such as difficulty breathing or loss of consciousness.
  • Follow the instructions of medical professionals and provide any relevant information about the snake bite.

After the Snake Bite:

  • Stay with the person until medical help arrives.
  • Reassure the person and try to keep them calm.
  • Do not apply a tourniquet or ice to the bitten area.
  • Avoid giving the person any medications or substances, unless instructed by medical professionals.
  • Follow any further instructions or recommendations given by medical professionals.
