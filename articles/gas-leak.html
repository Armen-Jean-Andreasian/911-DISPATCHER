Gas Leak

Emergency Preparedness Tips:

  • Install carbon monoxide detectors in your home and regularly test them.
  • Know the location of your gas shutoff valve and learn how to turn it off.
  • Ensure proper ventilation in areas where gas appliances are installed.
  • Avoid storing flammable materials near gas appliances or pipes.
  • Teach everyone in your household how to recognize the smell of gas.

What to Do During a Gas Leak:

  • Evacuate the premises immediately if you smell gas or suspect a leak.
  • Do not use any electrical devices or open flames, including light switches and matches.
  • Avoid creating sparks or generating static electricity.
  • Do not operate any gas appliances or attempt to locate the source of the leak.
  • Call emergency services and report the gas leak from a safe location.

After a Gas Leak:

  • Do not re-enter the premises until it has been declared safe by the authorities.
  • Have a professional inspect and repair the gas leak before using any gas appliances.
  • Avoid smoking or using open flames until the all-clear is given.
  • Seek necessary medical attention if anyone experiences symptoms of gas exposure.
  • Take steps to prevent future gas leaks, such as regular maintenance and inspections of gas appliances.
